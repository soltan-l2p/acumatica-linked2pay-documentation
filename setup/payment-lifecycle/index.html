Payment Lifecycle

Understand card and ACH authorization, capture, settlement, declines, voids, refunds, returns, recurring failures, duplicate prevention, and accounting effects.

Accounting state versus gateway state

Important: an Acumatica document being released does not always mean a card or ACH transaction has settled. Review both Acumatica status and linked2pay Payment Status/Webhook History.

The integration stores transaction ID, payment ID, status, last update, and history in L2PTransaction and L2PWebhookHistory.

Acumatica records and accounting entries

  • AR: AR Payment/Prepayment, applications, CCProcTran, linked2pay tracking/history, and cash account. Standard release normally debits cash and credits AR; verify the generated batch.
  • Fees: Apply Fee creates/releases an AR Debit Memo using CCFEE and applies it. Posting setup controls GL entries.
  • Voids/refunds: standard Acumatica documents create reversing entries when released; tracking remains tied to the original transaction.

Duplicate prevention

After a timeout, inspect CCProcTran, Payment Status, Webhook History, and the portal before retrying. Duplicate void/refund guards exist, but no universal idempotency key is documented for every initial payment request.

Reconciliation checklist

  • Acumatica and gateway amounts match.
  • Transaction/payment IDs recorded.
  • Status is final for the rail.
  • Cash account matches bank settlement.
  • GL batch posts to expected accounts/period.
  • Fees, returns, refunds, and chargebacks handled separately.
