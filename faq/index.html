Frequently asked questions

The answers controllers, CFOs, and AR managers need before a demo - in plain language.

Does the integration send vendor payments?

Not in the current release. linked2pay for Acumatica currently receives customer credit card and ACH payments through Accounts Receivable. Vendor payments are planned for a future release; do not scope, configure, demonstrate, or sell them as available today.

What exactly is linked2pay for Acumatica?

It is a payment integration installed in Acumatica. The current release supports Accounts Receivable customer credit card and ACH collections, saved payment methods, recurring billing, synchronization, fees, and reconciliation.

Does it support both credit cards and ACH?

Yes. Both are first-class payment methods with their own processing centers, saved (tokenized) accounts on the customer record, and full support in one-time and recurring payments.

Do payments sync back into Acumatica automatically?

The integration records gateway results against the Acumatica payment workflow, and webhooks update transaction status. Teams must still validate releases, cash accounts, settlement, returns, and generated batches during reconciliation.

Can it handle recurring invoices and auto-collection?

Yes. Create an invoice template, add it to a recurring schedule, and AutoPayBill generates and collects the invoices automatically against the customer's saved payment method.

How long does implementation take?

Setup is configuration-led: publish the validated customization, connect linked2pay, configure payment methods, and complete sandbox testing. A VAR or developer may be required for version validation, environment builds, network controls, or conflicts. The step-by-step setup guide covers every screen.

Will it reduce our processing costs?

Two ways: built-in processing fee programs can offset card acceptance costs automatically, and making ACH as easy as card shifts volume to the lowest-cost rail.

Is it secure? Where is card data stored?

Card and bank account details are tokenized and vaulted with linked2pay so Acumatica uses payment-profile references instead of raw account data. Tokenization reduces exposure but does not remove merchant PCI, access-control, or data-handling responsibilities.
